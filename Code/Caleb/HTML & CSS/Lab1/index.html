Oregon Icon

Ken Kesey, while born in Colorado, lived the majority of his life in Oregon. He is a literary & cultural legend, who in his own words, considered himself to be a link between the Beat Generation of the 1950s to the hippies of the 1960s.

book

Caleb's Favorite Author

Growing up, his novel, Sometimes a Great Notion, was my favorite book. To this day, I find immense appreciation for his masterful manner of telling a story. His style of writing is just as unique and iconic as his real-life exploits and endeavors.

Merry Prankster

He was one of the prominent members of the Merry Pranksters, a band of counterculture activists & artists who were most "noted for the sociological significanace of a lengthy raod trip they took in the summe rof 1964, traveling across the United States in a psychedelic painted school bus called Furthur or Further, organizing parties and giving out LSD. During this time they met many of the guiding lights of the mid-1960s cultural movement and presaged what are commonly thought of as hippies with odd behavior, tie-dyed and red, white and blue clothing, and renunciation of normal society, which they dubbed The Establishment."

Timeline of his life:

  • 1935: Born on September 17, in La Junta, Colorado.
  • 1946: The family moves to Springfield, Oregon.
  • 1953: He graduates from Springfield High School.
  • 1956: Kesey elopes with his high-school sweetheart, Norma "Faye" Haxby, whom he had met in seventh grade.
  • 1957: He graduates with a B.A. in speech and communication from the University of Oregon.
  • Late 1950s: Kesey moves to California and attends creative writing courses at Stanford University.
  • 1962: One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest is published to critical acclaim.
  • 1963: Kesey, with money from 'Sometimes a Great Notion', purchases a log house in La Honda, California
  • 1964: His second book, 'Sometimes a Great Notion', is published.
  • 1964: Kesey and the Merry Pranksters tour the country on their way to and from New York in 'Further'.
  • 1964-65: Kesey & the Merry Pranksters host many acid tests in the bay area.
  • 1965: Kesey is arrested in La Honda for possession of marijuna.
  • 1965-66: In attempt to evade the law, Kesey fakes his own suicide, hides in Mexico for 8 months, and returns back to California to be arrested.
  • 1966: Kesey serves six months in San Mateo County jail in Redwood City, California.
  • 1966: Kesey moves to Pleasant Hill, Oregon. Where he would remain in the Willamette Valley for the rest of his life.
